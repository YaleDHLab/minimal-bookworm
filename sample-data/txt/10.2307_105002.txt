Two Essays addressed to the Rev. James Bradley, D. D. and Astrom. Reg. by Mr. Charles Walmesley, F. R. S. Reverend Sir, I H A V E taken the liberty to address to you two little essays, that relate to astronomy; for as no one is more master of that science, or has enriched it with greater discoveries, than yourself, you can best judge of the worth and use of any performance in that kind. The first essay is a Theory on the Precession of the Equinoxes, and the Nutation of the Earth's Axis; which, as it is indebted to you for the discovery of the cause, on which it is founded, as also for the settling of the effects, with which its result is to be compared, ought to be laid before you as a homage, that of right is due. You expressed a desire of a theory on that subject: I have therefore examined, according to the principle of gravity, what motions may be produced in the globe Read Nov. 4, 1756. [701] globe of the earth by the actions of the sun and moon, and have endeavoured to determine their precise quantity and laws of variation. You observed yourself, that the supposition you made use of, of the earth's pole moving round the periphery of a circle, whose center represented the mean place of the pole, was not exact: and in effect, as theory shews there are two equations arising from the sun's action, and as many from the action of the moon, to be used in settling the true place of the pole, the simple motion in the circle cannot answer accurately to the composition of these several motions; and it is from thence proceeded that surprizing difference you found betwixt the polar distances calculated on that supposition, and those observed, in the star ~ Cassiopea, in the year 1738, and in ~ ursae majoris in the year 1740 and 1741; which distances, if computed from the theory, as here laid down, agree with the observations as nearly as the others. This appears in the tables that are added of these computations. You also insinuated it would be proper to examine, whether the position of the moon's apogee had not a share of influence in these apparent motions of the stars. I therefore considered that point, but found, as you will see in the fifth proposition, that the diminution of the moon's action in the higher part of its orbit is so compensated by the increase of the same action in the lower part, that in the whole revolution of the moon no alteration arises, whatever be the situation of the nodes. The second essay is a Theory of the Irregularities, that may be occasioned in the annual Motion of the Earth by the Actions of Jupiter and Saturn. I was VOL. 49. 4 U led [702] led into this research by reflecting upon that question, debated among the astronomers for so many ages past, whether the mean inclination of the two planes of the ecliptic and equator suffers any change or remains invariable. Considering then what cause could produce a change in this inclination, I easily conceived, that if the action of Jupiter had sufficient power to alter the plane of the earth's orbit, with respect to its own, by making their common intersection recede, in the same manner as the sun's action operates on the lunar orbit, an alteration in the obliquity of the ecliptic would necessarily follow; and upon closer examination it appeared, that Jupiter really caused the earth to deviate in its course, and gave a retrograde motion to the line of intersection of their orbits; and further, that according to the present situation of that line, its regress was such, as to have occasioned a gradual diminution in the obliquity of the ecliptic for many ages past: by which means that question seems decided. The reason, why the astronomers have not hitherto been able to settle that point, is, because this variation proceeds at so slow a rate, that the observations of the ancients are not sufficiently exact to ascertain the small diminution, that has happened since their time. I have endeavoured to fix the laws, quantity and period of this variation. From the same cause are also computed a progressive motion occasioned in the earth's aphely, and a small regressive one in the equinoctial points: in all which is added the little share of influence, that belongs to Saturn. In the last proposition are deduced some inequalities, that occur in certain elements of the earth's theory, that have hitherto been supposed [703] supposed invariable. These, as they are very small, I have only added in that view, that you, who know the best what degree of precision may be expected from astronomical observations, may judge whether they are worth notice or not. I must observe, that some of the points of these two treatises have been considered by others; and if my conclusions any where differ from them, I leave it to other geometricians to decide which are right. All I shall say on that head is, that my result agrees with the computation of the great Sir Isaac Newton. As to the method, I have rather chosen to deduce the propositions by geometrical reasoning, after the manner of Sir Isaac Newton; which in researches of this kind always appeared to me much more simple, more rational, and more elegant, than the long calculus of an intricate analysis. Besides, if in the application there slips any error, it is more easily discovered in the first method. As a lover of the sciences, I should be glad to contribute to their improvement; but, whether what is here offered may be reputed a step that way, is left intirely to your determination. Describat luna in plano Eclipticae ellipsim AP B L (Fig. 6.) cujus centrum sit C, T focus quem Terra occupat, A B axis major, C D semiaxis minor, T L communis sectio planorum AEquatoris et Eclipticae. Esto Luna in P, et ducantur TP, Tp quae abscindant sectorem T P p motu lunae horario descriptum. Centro T et radio semiaxi majori C A aequali describatur circulus H N O secans T P et T p in N et n, atque in T L demittantur perpendicula N I, n m, et in [723] in T A perpendiculum N R. Si luna in circulo H N O revolvi supponeretur, ubi ad locum N pertingerit, praecessio horaria aequinoctiorum vi lunae genita foret, per demonstrata in propositione prima, ut N I2; at praecessio illa crescit in ratione vis qua gignitur, et haec vis est in ratione triplicata inversa distantiae lunae T P, adeoque praecessio horaria est ut N I2/T P3 sive ut eadem quantitas N I2/T P3 ducta in sectorem constantem T P p, hoc est, ut NI2xNn/TP sive ut NIxIm/TP; sed ex natura ellipseos habetur 1/TP = CA2+TCxTR/C A x CD2: unde tota praecessio genita quo tempore luna in orbe suo revolvitur est ut summa quantitatum N I x I m x C A2 + T C x T R/C A x C D2 in circulo, sive (quia rejici potest terminus ambiguus + T C x T R/C A x C D2, utpote per alteram dimidiam circumferentia circuli partem positivus, per alteram dimidiam negativus) ut summa omnium in circulo factorum N I x I m, hoc est, ut area ipsa circuli H N O H; ac proinde Praecessio AEquinoctiorum in singulis lunae revolutionibus manet eadem in quolibet Apogaei situ. Variatio horaria inclinationis Eclipticae, si luna existeret in N revolvendo in circulo H N O, foret ex demonstratis in prop. 3. ut N I x T I: si vero transferatur luna in P, eadem variatio erit ut N I x T I/T P3 vel ut [724] ut N I x T I/T P3 x T P p, hoc est, ut N I x T I x N n/T P sive, ducta nq parallela T I, ut N I x n q/T P; proindeque, ob rationem mox datam, variatio Inclinationis Eclipticae tempore revolutionis lunae genita est ut summa omnium in circulo factorum N I x n q, id est, nulla. Ex praecedentibus liquet Terrae Polis geminos motus competere ab utrisque seorsim Solis et Lunae, quatenus extra AEquatorem revolventium, viribus oriundos; alterum plano Eclipticae parallelum, quo puncta AEquinoctialia in antecedentia continuo retrahuntur, ac propterea stellae promoveri videntur in consequentia. Motus alter est ad planum Eclipticae perpendicularis, quo Terrae Poli nutant et oscillantur accedendo ad polos Eclipticae et ab eis recedendo per vices, atque inde mutatur Declinatio stellarum. Horum motuum quantitatem directe deduximus ab excessu altitudinis terrae ad AEquatorem supra altitudinem ejus ad polos, secundum duplicem hypothesim, qua nempe excessus ille aestimatur pars 1/230 vel 1/178 altitudinis totius, quae hactenus est a Mathematicis potissimum usurpata. Si vero nota praesupponatur Nutatio terrae axis, quae quatenus actioni lunae debita statuatur aequalis 18~, et inde quaerantur motus reliqui, per propositiones supra traditas ii prodeunt, praecessio scilicet aequinoctiorum annua mediocris 5 [725] cris vi solis genita 16~. 24~, vi lunae 33~. 54~, aequatio praecessionis maxima vi solis 1~. 23~, vi lunae 16~. 45~: Nutatio axis vi solis 1~. 10~, manente nimirum terra uniformiter densa. Si nulla habeatur ratio aequationum, quas in Praecessione AEquinoctiorum et Nutatione axis terrae generat vis solis, consequitur ex prop. 2. et 4. motum Poli terrestris satis accurate fieri in ellipsi, cujus axis major, qui jacet in plano Coluri Solstitiorum, est aequalis 19~1/2 et axis minor 141/2, atque angulum describere circa centrum ellipseos aequalem motui nodi lunaris. Forte arguet quis hypothesim, quam de densitate terrae uniformi, simulque de ejusdem diametrorum ratione 178/177 libere usurpavimus, cum utrumque una consistere non possit. Equidem, si ad rerum cognitionem [733] tionem summam attingere fas esset, Theoriam inde perfectam evadere non diffitemur. Sed, praeterquam quod quaenam sit accurata diametrorum ratio et constitutio interna globi terraquei hactenus non constet, atque etiam taedio nimis esset omnes, qui possunt casus diversae densitatis excogitari, sigillatim discutere; non sequitur labefactari praecedentem theoriam, etiam fi forte verum sit terram non esse uniformiter densam, neque proportionem diametrorum esse eam, quam adhibuimus. Nam, dato AEquinoctiorum motu medio a vi solis vel lunae oruindo, patet ex propositionibus praecedentibus rite inde determinari aequationes Praecessionis et Nutationis, quippe quae in quacumque densitatis hypothesi semper sunt proportionales praedicto motui medio, et legem constantem servant. Unde, si vel AEquinoctiorum Praecessionem vel axis terrae Nutationem ipsam, quae revera est, assumpsimus, quantumvis simus de terrae configuratione hallucinati, vera omnia et firma consistere videtur. SCHOLIUM III. Quanquam Poli Terrestris evagationes, qua potuimus perspicuitate, ex suis causis deduximus ac demonstravimus; theoriam tamen ipsam constructione geometrica breviter illustrare non pigebit, cum unica ad eas, quae a sole pendent, altera ad illas, quae a luna, exhibendas constructio sufficiat. In circulo LRG (Fig. 7.) cujus centrum T, ducantur radii duo TL, TR, ad se invicem normales, et in TR sumpto puncto V, ita ut sit TV ad RV ut motus solis medius ad motum medium aequinoctiorum vi solis genitum, centro T et semiaxibus TL, TV deVOL. 49. 5 A cribatur [734] cribatur ellipsis LVG; atque hoc pacto erit motus solis medius ad motum solis medium ab aequinoctio ut area ellipseos ad aream circuli, TR ad RV ut tangens obliquitatis Eclipticae mediocris ad tangentem variationis totius ejusdem Obliquitatis. Et si exhibeat T terram, L punctum aequinoctiale, et in circulo ducatur radius quilibet TS ellipsim secans in P, erit motus aequinoctiorum ad motum solis medium, quo tempore sol ab aequinoctio degreditur per arcum LS, ut spatium SLP ad sectorem ellipticum PTL, et RV ad SP ut tangens variationis totius obliquitatis eclipticae ad tangentem variationis tempore praedicto factae, sive ut ipsa variatio prior ad variationem posteriorem quam proxime. Item ducta ad circulum recta P F parallela rectae T R, cum sit angulus STL distantia solis vera ab aequinoctio, erit angulus FTL distantia ejusdem media, atque adeo erit angulus FTS aequatio motus aequinoctiorum, et sinus hujus anguli, ubi maximus est inoctantibus aequinoctiorum, est ad radium ut RV ad TR + TV, ex natura ellipseos; in aliis locis ejusdem aequationis sinus, vel etiam ipsa aequatio, est ut sinus duplae distantiae solis ab aequinoctio vel solstitio quam proxime. Ut haec demonstrentur, motus solis ponatur uniformis, et recta TS ferri intelligatur circa centrum T cum summa velocitatum solis et aequinoctii, atque in data temporis particula describat sectorem STs: hoc pacto si recta Ts secet ellipsim in p, et ducatur SH perpendicularis in TL, ex natura hujus ellipseos datur sector PTp, et areola SPps est ut SH2, id est, ut quadratum sinus distantiae solis ab aequinoctio, atque in eadem ratione est etiam linea SP quam proxime. Conferantur haec cum demonstratis [735] stratis in prop. 3. et in Coroll. 2. prop. 1, et patebit constructio. Hic autem motum aequinoctiorum vi lunae debitum negligo, quia parvi momenti est; sin ejus habeatur ratio, pro motu medio solis substitui debet summa motus medii solis et motus medii aequinoctii vi lunae geniti. Jam inaequalitates eae, quae pendent a fitu nodorum lunae, ita fere exhiberi possunt. Circuli E A G (Fig. 8.) radius T E dividatur in C, ita ut fit T E ad T C ut motus nodi ab aequinoctio ad motum aequinoctii vi lunae genitum, et ut radius ad sinum inclination's orbis lunaris ad Eclipticam conjunctim, atque centro C, foco T, et semiaxe majore CB = TE describatur ellipsis B A D. Tum si area tota circuli E A G E exponat revolutionem nodi ad idem aequinoctium, area B A E sive A D G diminuta in ratione radii ad tangentem duplicatae inclinationis Eclipticae ad AEquatorem exprimet aequationem nodorum maximam quamproxime, et recta B E aequalis erit sinui aequationis maximae Obliquitatis Eclipticae ad radium T E. Insuper si T denotet terram, E punctum aequinoctii verni, et ad locum nodi ducatur recta T P occurrens circulo in N et ellipsi in P, aequatio aequinoctiorum eo tempore ad erit aequationem maximam ut spatium B P N E ad spatium B A E, et aequatio Obliquitatis Eclipticae ad aequationem maximam ut recta P N ad rectam B E. Ubi nodus ultra Solstitium digressus pervenerit in n, ducto radio T n secante el ipsim in p, aequatio aequinoctiorum eo in casu proportionalis est differentiae spatiorum A B E, A n p, atque aequatio Obliquitatis Eclipticae proportionalis linea n p fit negativa. Cum enim perexigua sit excentricitas T C, ex natura ellipseos spatium A B E sive A D G, producto scilicet axe majore B D 5 A 2 donec [736] donec secet circulum in G, aequatur facto T E x T C quam proxime, et ducta N H perpendiculari in T E, est spatium B P N E ut N H et recta P N ut T H. His igitur collatis cum iis quae demonstrata sunt in prop. 2 et 4, palam fiet constructio. Hic monitum volo, quod initio fieri oportuit, per motum solis vel nodi medium, de quo toties est sermo in propositionibus, intelligi debere motum solis vel nodi medium ab aequinoctio, id est, motum compositum ex motuum mediocrium vel summa solis et aequinoctii, vel differentia nodi et aequinoctii. Quamvis enim motus ille aequinoctii tantillus sit prae motu solis vel nodi, ut in computo aequationum praecessionis aequinoctiorum vel nutationis axis terrae nullum ejus omissio inducat errorem sensibilem, hoc eo cavetur, ut accurata procedat propositionum demonstratio. Denique Orbitae Lunaris ad Eclipticam inclinationem constantem supponere non dubitavi, licet variabilis sit; siquidem, cum variatio illa sit paucorum minutorum, atque adeo aequationem nonnisi perexiguam hic generare valeat, hujusmodi minutiis Theoriam implicare atque onerare nolui. C. Walmesley. De [737] De Inaequalitatibus motuum Terrae. QUIBUS in motu suo Tellus nostra ob actionem Lunae inaequalitatibus subjaceat, ab aliis jam fere expensum habetur. Quae vero perturbationes ex viribus planetarum reliquorum oriri possint, quia vix quidquam delibatum reperitur, ideo visum fuit harum investigationem juxta principia Gravitatis Newtonianae instituere. Actiones quidem Mercurii, Veneris et Martis, ob horum corporum parvitatem et vires ignotas, praetermittimus; atque adeo ad solas Jovis et Saturni, praesertim Jovis planetarum omnium maximi, disquisitio nostra restringitur. Plana autem orbium horum planetarum, licet ob mutuas actiones non penitus immota, in sequentibus tamen tanquam immota supponere fas erit, cum tantilla mutatio in motum terrae vix influere possit. Hic non investigatur motus puncti AEquinoctialis, quatenus AEquator terrae ob materiam ibi redundantem vi Jovis et Saturni mutaret situm suum respectu Eclipticae, quemadmodum viribus Solis et Lunae fieri innotescit; hujusmodi enim mutatio ex actionibus Jovis vel Saturni oriunda omnino debet esse insensibilis: sed motum illum AEquinoctii quaerimus, qui oritur ex variatione, quam fieri in situ plani Eclipticae supra monstravimus. Errorum angularium AEquationes maximae, cum et ipsae sint errores angulares, sunt directe ut vires et quadrata temporum, quibus generantur, conjunctim, et inverse ut orbium diametri; ideoque sunt ut ipsi errores sive motus, quorum sunt aequationes, temporibus istis geniti: tempora autem ipsa sunt quamproxime ut aequationum periodi. Unde ob datos motuum lunarium et terrestrium errores, aequationumque [755] numque periodos, ex datis errorum lunarium aequationibus per analogiam eruentur aequationes errorum terrestrium. Sic, periodus aequationis Apogaei lunaris et Variationis AEquationis centri lunae cum sit proportionalis revolutioni solis ad Apogaeum lunae, ac propterea ob similitudinem virium similiter applicatarum periodus aequationis Aphelii terrae et Variationis aequationis centri proportionalis esse debeat revolutioni Jovis ad terrae Aphelium, erunt aequationes istae lunares ad aequationes hasce terrae similes, ut motus Apogaei lunaris tempore revolutionis solis ad lunae apogaeum, ad motum Aphelii terrae tempore revolutionis Jovis ad ipsum terrae Aphelium, hoc est, existente motu medio Apogaei lunaris annuo 40~. 40~. 43~ et motu annuo Aphelii terrae supra invento 13~. 2~. 28iv, ut 45~. 51~. 40~ ad 2~. 34~. 42~. Quare posita variatione tota aequationis maximae centri lunae aequali 2~. 41~1/2 prout fere habetur in tabulis Astronomicis, erit variatio aequationis maximae centri Terrae sive Solis 9~. 4~. Denotet igitur AE aequationem centri solis maximam mediocrem, eritque AE+4~. 32~ aequatio maxima, et AE-4~. 32~ aequatio minima; atque his aequationibus dabuntur etiam excentricitates congruae. Tum, quemadmodum variatio aequationis maximae centri lunae crescit in ratione duplicata sinus distantiae Apogaei lunaris a quadraturis suis cum sole, ita variatio aequationis maximae centri solis, id est, incrementum aequationis minimae augetur in ratione duplicata sinus distantiae aphelii terrae a quadraturis suis cum Jove: sive, variatio aequationis mediae est ad semissem [756] missem variationis totius, nempe ad 4~. 32~, ut cosinus duplae distantiae Jovis ab Aphelio terrae ad radium; et additur aequationi mediae, ubi linea Apsidum Orbis magni pergit ab octantibus suis cum Jove ad syzgyias, vel a syzygiis ad octantes; in reliqua parte subducitur. Utrum autem tantilla variatio Observationibus patere possit, Astronomis definiendum relinquo. Haud secus, si aequatio maxima apogaei lunae statuatur 12~. 18~ erit 45~. 51~. 40~ ad 2~. 34~. 42~ ut 12~. 18~ ad aequationem maximam motus Aphelii terrae sive Apogaei solis, quae proinde erit 41~, 30~, ubi scilicet Apsides Orbis Telluris versantur in octantibus suis cum Jove. In aliis positionibus aequatio Aphelii erit ad aequationem maximam ut sinus duplae distantiae Jovis ab aphelio terrae ad radium; motui vero medio additur in transitu apsidum orbis magni a syzygiis suis cum Jove ad quadraturas, et in transitu a quadraturis ad syzygias subducitur, ac proinde in casu quolibet habebitur verus Aphelii terrae sive Apogaei solis locus. Hoc pacto confecimus tabulam sequentem, si forte usui esse possit, in qua AE denotat aequationem centri solis maximam mediocrem.

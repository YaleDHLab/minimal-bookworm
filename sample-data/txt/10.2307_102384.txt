(659) IV. Extract of a Letter from Mr. Scarburgh, dated Acomack, July 14. 1694. Giving a Relation of the Effects of a violent Storm of the 19th of October, on the Rivers of that Country. Communicated by the Honourable Sir Robert Southwell, F. R. S. U P O N the 19th Day of October last happened a most violent Storm, which stopped the Course of ancient Channels, and made some where never were any: So that betwixt the Bounds of Virginia and New-Castle (in Pensylvania) on the Sea-bord side, are many Navigable Rivers for Sloops and small Vessels.

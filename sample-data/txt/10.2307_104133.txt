[20] VI. An Observation of extraordinary Warmth of the Air in January 1741 2. communicated in a Letter from the Revd Mr. H. Miles to Mr. John Eames, F. R. S. Tooting, Surry, Jan. 20. 1741 2. M Y Mercurial Thermometer abroad, was last Night, at 10 o'Clock, 20 Degrees above the freezing Point; which is higher than it was sixteen Mornings of the one-and-thirty in May last, and higher than in any Morning in April, one excepted. Read Jan. 21. 1741 2.

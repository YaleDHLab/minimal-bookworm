A Note. WHereas Numb. 64. p.2071.l.27. mention is made of a Letter of Dr. Wallis about the Moons Perigaeum in the matter of Tydes; the Reader may be pleased to take this further notice, That that mention is to be understood to have been made in the Latine Version given by the said Doctor, of his Answer to the Objections to his Hypothesis; (for in the English, Printed Numb.16, 'tis not:) In which Version sent long agoe by way of Letter to the Publisher, are these words; (to be inserted after those of Num.16.p.286.l.11. viz.The Niceties are to be adjusted in time, from particular Observation;) Et quidem, si ad minutias descendere libuisset, habenda fuisset ratio, tum Apogaei & Perigaei Lunae, tum Obliquitatis Orbitae Lunaris ad Eclipticam comparatae, & Inter sectionum, quas Caput & Caudam Draconis dicunt. Sed & monendum fuisset, Terrae Motum Annuum atque Diurnum, cum non eundem seu parallelos Axes habeant, se mutuo nunc magis nunc minus intersecare: Item, Inundationes Nili, aliorumque aliquot fluviorum, statis Anni temporibus factas, ad huiusmodi forte causam referri posse: fortassis & statos ventos.Sed haec intacta plane praetereo, aliaque huiusmodi multa; quae tamen, si quando ad particularia deveniendum erit, con deranda erunt.

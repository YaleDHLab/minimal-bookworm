IV. An Observation of the Eclipse of the Sun on May 2, 1733, in the Afternoon. By Mr. George Graham, F. R. S. in Fleetstreet, London. Made with a Telescope of ten Feet in Length, fitted with a Micrometer. App. Time. At 5h 44~ 45~ It began. 6 25 30 The Cusps were vertical. 6 37 30 The Eclipse was greatest, the lucid Part of the Sun's Diameter measuring 426 Parts, whereof the Sun's Diameter measured 2311. So that the Eclipse was 9 4/5 Digits. 6 46 00 The Cusps were horizontal. 7 28 23 The Eclipse ended. V. A

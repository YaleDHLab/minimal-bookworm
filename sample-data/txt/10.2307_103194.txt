This is thicker, softer, and more loose than our common Crested Grass, and in spike more nearly resembles our yellow Spring Grass: Dr Zuinger, Physician, and Son to the famous Professor of that Name, a very accurate Botanist, hath lately, amongst other curious Plants, sent me this Grass, which he gathered on the hilly Meadows about Basil, where Caspar Bauhine first discovered it. He says it grows with the common, but more rarely; the same found in the Woods there, he observes to sport or vary in its spike, viz. some more loose and greenish, others thicker and yellowish; the Leaves and Stalks in some are hairy, in others smooth.

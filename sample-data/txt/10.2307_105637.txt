The body of the observatory (erected at the Cape) was circular, the radius of which 61/2 feet in the clear; the height of the circular wall 51/2 feet; the roof conical, and moveable, (made of board) a lid in it of 3 feet breadth, to open, which was easily turned to any part of the heavens, as the whole top moved freely. The clock was fixed against two pieces of timber (let near 4 feet into the ground) of 10 inches by 8; these pieces being joined together by pins of 11/2 inch diameter.

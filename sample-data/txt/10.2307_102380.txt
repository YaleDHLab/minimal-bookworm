(632) V. Extract of a Letter from Mr. de Vallemont, &c. concerning a small Egg being found within an ordinary one, taken from the Journal des Savans of the 7th of Jan. 1696. IN the Journal des Scavans of the 7th of Jan. 1696. is an account of a small Egg of about 7 Lines from end to end, and 41/2 of bigness, which was included in a Hens Egg, which appeared to have nothing extraordinary on its outside, and which they were breaking to make an Ommelette of. The small Egg-shell was fastened to the shell of the greater by one of its Extremities. Several Instances of the like nature are extant in Natural Historians. London: Printed for Sam. Smith, and Benj. Wallford, Printers to the Royal Society, at the Princes Arms in St. Paul's Church-Yard. 1697.

The Appearance of a fiery Meteor, as seen by Mr. Cradock, communicated to the Royal Society by Mr. Henry Baker, F. R. S. T H E Head and Body emitted an extremely lucid and white Flame. The Tail appeared of a transparent Blue, like the Flame of Sulphur. Read June 7. 1744. This Phaenomenon was seen on Sunday, May 27. 1744. at 11 Minutes after 11 o' Clock at Night: Its Direction from S. E. to N. W. or thereabouts; its Height seemingly not half a Mile. It was seen, as here described, from the Terrace in Somerset-Gardens, by me, Zach. Cradock, Of Somerset-House.
